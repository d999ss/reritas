We start with a lab test to see what your hormones and metabolic markers are actually doing, and meet with your licensed clinician on video to review your symptoms and results together. After everything is taken into consideration, if treatment makes sense, you can decide on a medication and plan that works for you.

The best option is the one that fits your labs, symptoms, goals, and lifestyle. That's why we start with testing first. Your medical provider walks you through what makes sense for your body. With flat, all-inclusive pricing, you choose what's right for you. Plans start at just $139/month.

Enclomiphene helps your body make more of its own testosterone by improving the brain-to-testes signal, so it's often the top choice when fertility matters. TRT replaces testosterone directly from an external source (oral pills, topical cream, or injections). Because TRT can reduce your natural production and sperm count, your physician uses your labs and goals to determine the right path.

At this time, we don't accept outside bloodwork. All baseline and follow-up labs must be completed through our approved testing process. This ensures accuracy and consistency, so we can compare results measured in the exact same way and timeframe without introducing variables that could affect how changes are interpreted.

We hold ourselves to the highest scientific standards

  1. A large U.S. population study found ~25% of adult men have total testosterone below 300 ng/dL, a standard clinical threshold for low testosterone. (D Liu et al., BMC Public Health 2024).

  2. In a large U.S. commercial insurance claims analysis (MarketScan, 2008–2017), only 20.8% of men diagnosed with hypogonadism received testosterone therapy in the most recent year studied (J Auerbach et al., International Journal of Impotence Research 2022).

  3. A major population study found that age-matched men today have substantially lower testosterone than men the same age a generation ago—about a 22% decline over ~17 years. (T Travison et al., The Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism 2007).
