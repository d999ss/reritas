Viagra for ED: Dosage, Side Effects & How It Works (2026 Guide)

Viagra® is one of the most recognizable erectile dysfunction (ED) medications in the world. Since its approval in the late 1990s, Viagra has helped millions of men improve erection strength, reliability, and sexual confidence.

While Viagra remains a well-known brand, most prescriptions today are filled using its generic equivalent, sildenafil. At the same time, modern combination treatments have emerged that build on Viagra’s strengths while addressing limitations related to duration, onset consistency, and mental arousal.

This guide covers everything you need to know about Viagra for ED, including how it works, available dosages, common side effects, how it compares to other ED medications, how generic sildenafil differs from brand-name Viagra, and how newer combination options like Rugiet Ready support more complete sexual performance.

What Is Viagra?

Viagra is a prescription medication used to treat erectile dysfunction by increasing blood flow to the penis. It belongs to a class of drugs called PDE5 inhibitors, which relax blood vessels and improve circulation during sexual arousal.

Key facts about Viagra:

  • Viagra does not cause spontaneous erections
  • Sexual stimulation is still required
  • It primarily treats the physical blood-flow component of ED
  • Viagra’s active ingredient is sildenafil

Although Viagra was originally available only as a brand-name medication, sildenafil is now widely prescribed in generic form through telehealth providers.

How Viagra Works

When a man becomes sexually aroused, nitric oxide is released in the body. This triggers a process that relaxes blood vessels in the penis, allowing increased blood flow.

Viagra works by blocking the PDE5 enzyme, allowing improved circulation to erectile tissue and helping men achieve and maintain firmer erections during sexual activity. Viagra does not directly increase libido or sexual desire.

Viagra Dosages and Their Effects

Viagra is typically prescribed in three dosage strengths. The appropriate dose depends on age, health, tolerance, and individual response.

25 mg Viagra

  • Often prescribed for first-time users
  • Lower risk of side effects
  • May be less effective for moderate or severe ED

50 mg Viagra

  • Most commonly prescribed starting dose
  • Balances effectiveness and tolerability
  • Works well for many men

100 mg Viagra

  • Maximum recommended dose
  • Higher likelihood of side effects
  • Not always more effective than lower doses

Increasing dosage does not guarantee better results and may increase side effects.

Common Side Effects of Viagra

Common side effects include:

  • Headache
  • Facial flushing
  • Nasal congestion
  • Indigestion
  • Sensitivity to light

Side effects are usually mild and temporary.

Viagra vs Other ED Medications

Viagra vs Cialis

  • Viagra works faster (30–60 minutes)
  • Cialis lasts up to 36 hours

Viagra vs Levitra or Stendra

  • Similar or faster onset
  • Shorter duration
  • Less commonly prescribed

All PDE5 inhibitors primarily address blood flow and do not directly support mental arousal.

Generic Sildenafil vs Viagra

Viagra and sildenafil contain the same active ingredient and work the same way. The main differences are cost, branding, and access. Generic sildenafil is significantly more affordable and widely available through telehealth platforms.

Why Viagra or Sildenafil Alone Isn’t Always Enough

Erectile dysfunction is not always caused by circulation alone. Some men experience difficulty maintaining erections, delayed arousal, reduced desire, or performance anxiety despite adequate blood flow.
