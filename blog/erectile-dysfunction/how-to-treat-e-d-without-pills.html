2. Chewable Tablets

Chewable tablets offer a pill-free way to take sildenafil or tadalafil. However, they come with some notable drawbacks: they don't improve onset time compared to pills, they're still affected by food intake, and the bitter taste of the active ingredients often requires water to wash away. For many men, the experience isn't meaningfully better than taking a pill.

3. Penile Injections (Trimix)

If oral and sublingual options aren't providing enough support, penile injections вЂ” most commonly Trimix вЂ” are a highly effective next step. While more invasive than any oral treatment, they work directly and reliably even in cases of more severe E.D.

If you've exhausted non-invasive pharmaceutical options without success, speak with your doctor or urologist about whether Trimix is appropriate for you.

4. Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT)

In some cases, E.D. is driven by low testosterone rather than vascular issues alone. Testosterone plays a key role in libido, erection quality, and overall sexual function вЂ” and when levels are below normal, everything suffers.

Testosterone replacement therapy (TRT) can help restore hormonal balance and, in turn, improve erectile function. If you suspect low testosterone may be a factor, talk to your doctor or explore a reputable telehealth program like HendRx to find out if TRT is right for you.

Lifestyle Changes That Improve Erectile Function

Here's the truth: getting to a strong baseline of health will optimize every area of your life вЂ” including your sex life. Pharmaceutical treatments work best when your body is working with them, not against them. Here are the most impactful lifestyle changes for improving vascular health and reducing E.D. symptoms.

Regular Exercise

Cardiovascular activity вЂ” walking, jogging, cycling, swimming вЂ” increases blood flow throughout the body, including to the penis. It also lowers blood pressure, reduces body weight, and improves cholesterol levels, all of which contribute to healthier erections over time.

A Heart-Healthy Diet

A diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats supports vascular function at every level. Foods high in antioxidants вЂ” berries, leafy greens, nuts вЂ” help reduce inflammation and keep blood vessels functioning optimally.

Quitting Smoking

Smoking damages blood vessels and directly impairs blood flow, including to the penis. If there's one lifestyle change that will have the most immediate impact on E.D. and overall health, this is it.

Limiting Alcohol

Heavy alcohol consumption raises blood pressure, damages blood vessels, and impairs liver function вЂ” all of which can contribute to or worsen E.D. Reducing intake or cutting it out altogether can make a meaningful difference in both vascular health and sexual performance.

Managing Stress

Chronic stress increases blood pressure, constricts blood vessels, and disrupts hormonal balance вЂ” a perfect storm for E.D. Incorporating stress management practices like mindfulness meditation, yoga, or breathwork into your routine can lower baseline stress and support better sexual function.

Sauna Use and Cold Plunges

Contrast hydrotherapy вЂ” alternating between heat exposure in a sauna and cold plunges вЂ” has been shown to improve circulation and vascular health. Saunas dilate blood vessels and increase blood flow, while cold exposure stimulates circulation in a complementary way. Adding these to your routine may provide meaningful support for erectile function over time.
