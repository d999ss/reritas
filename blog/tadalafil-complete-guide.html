Tadalafil for ED: Dosage, Side Effects & How It Works (2026 Guide)

Contents
March 6, 2026

Tadalafil (Generic for Cialis) is one of the most commonly prescribed erectile dysfunction (ED) medications in the world. Known for its long duration and flexible dosing options, tadalafil has helped millions of men improve erection quality, reduce performance pressure, and regain sexual confidence.

Today, tadalafil is widely prescribed through telehealth providers in both daily and on-demand formats. At the same time, newer combination treatments have emerged that build on tadalafil’s strengths while addressing certain limitations related to onset speed and mental arousal.

This guide covers everything you need to know about tadalafil, including how it works, available dosages, onset timing, common side effects, how it compares to other PDE5 inhibitors, and how modern combination treatments like Rugiet Ready use tadalafil alongside sildenafil and apomorphine for more complete sexual performance support.

What Is Tadalafil?

Tadalafil is a prescription medication used to treat erectile dysfunction by increasing blood flow to the penis. It belongs to a class of drugs known as PDE5 inhibitors, which help relax blood vessels and improve circulation during sexual arousal.

Key facts about tadalafil:

  • Tadalafil does not cause spontaneous erections
  • Sexual stimulation is still required
  • It is most commonly prescribed in generic form
  • It is the active ingredient in Cialis®, but generic tadalafil is widely available through telehealth platforms like Rugiet, Hims, Ro, and several others

How Tadalafil Works

When a man becomes sexually aroused, the body releases nitric oxide. This triggers a chain reaction that relaxes smooth muscle tissue in the penis and allows increased blood flow.

Tadalafil works by:

  • Blocking the PDE5 enzyme
  • Sustaining smooth muscle relaxation
  • Supporting consistent blood flow to erectile tissue

Unlike faster-acting PDE5 inhibitors, tadalafil is absorbed more gradually and remains active in the body for an extended period of time. This long half-life is what allows tadalafil to last up to 36 hours.

Tadalafil primarily supports the physical blood-flow component of erections. It does not directly increase libido, desire, or mental arousal.

Tadalafil Dosages, Onset Time, and Effects

Tadalafil can be prescribed in daily or on-demand dosing formats. In addition to dosage strength, onset timing and duration vary depending on how tadalafil is used.

The appropriate dose depends on individual response, tolerance, frequency of sexual activity, and overall health.

2.5 mg Tadalafil (Daily)

  • Lowest daily maintenance dose
  • Taken every day, not timed to sex
  • Builds steady levels in the body over time
  • Provides baseline erectile support
  • May be insufficient for moderate or severe ED

Onset timing:

  • Effects are gradual, not immediate
  • Requires several days of consistent use
  • Not designed for same-day or on-demand results

5 mg Tadalafil (Daily)

  • Most commonly prescribed daily dose
  • Taken once daily regardless of sexual activity
  • Maintains continuous erectile support
  • Allows spontaneity without planning
  • Lower peak intensity than on-demand dosing

Onset timing:

  • Initial effects may appear after 2–5 days
  • Full effectiveness often reached after 5–7 days of daily use
  • Not intended for rapid, same-day activation

10 mg Tadalafil (On-Demand)

  • Taken before sexual activity
  • Balanced effectiveness and tolerability
  • Often prescribed for first-time tadalafil users

Onset timing:

  • Typically begins working in 30–60 minutes
  • Onset may be delayed by food or slower metabolism
  • Remains active for up to 36 hours

20 mg Tadalafil (On-Demand)

  • Maximum recommended dose
  • Strongest effects and longest duration
  • Higher likelihood of side effects
  • Not always more effective than lower doses

Onset timing:

  • Begins working in 30–60 minutes for most men
  • Some may experience effects closer to 1–2 hours
  • Provides extended responsiveness for up to 36 hours

Important Notes on Tadalafil Onset

  • Increasing dosage does not guarantee faster onset
  • Daily tadalafil prioritizes consistency, not speed
  • On-demand tadalafil prioritizes duration, not rapid activation
  • Faster onset generally requires non-oral delivery methods

Common Side Effects of Tadalafil

Tadalafil side effects are usually mild to moderate and tend to be dose-dependent.

Common side effects include:

  • Headache
  • Facial flushing
  • Nasal congestion
  • Indigestion
  • Muscle or back pain

Muscle and back aches are more commonly reported with tadalafil than with some other PDE5 inhibitors due to its longer duration in the body.

Most side effects are temporary and resolve on their own.

Tadalafil vs Other PDE5 Inhibitors

Tadalafil is one of several PDE5 inhibitors used to treat erectile dysfunction. Each option has distinct characteristics.

Tadalafil vs Sildenafil

  • Tadalafil lasts up to 36 hours
  • Sildenafil typically lasts 4–6 hours
  • Tadalafil is often taken daily or far in advance
  • Sildenafil is taken closer to sexual activity
  • Tadalafil prioritizes duration and flexibility
  • Sildenafil prioritizes faster onset

Tadalafil vs Vardenafil

  • Similar onset to sildenafil
  • Shorter duration than tadalafil
  • Less commonly prescribed

Tadalafil vs Avanafil

  • Faster onset than tadalafil
  • Shorter durationOften more expensive and less widely available

All PDE5 inhibitors primarily focus on blood flow and do not directly support mental arousal or sexual desire.

Why Tadalafil Alone Isn’t Always Enough

While tadalafil improves circulation and erection reliability, some men continue to experience performance challenges related to:

  • Low sexual desire
  • Anxiety or performance pressure
  • Delayed mental arousal
  • Inconsistent responsiveness

In these cases, improving blood flow alone may not fully address the root cause of ED symptoms.

How Tadalafil and Sildenafil Work Together

When properly formulated and prescribed, tadalafil and sildenafil can complement each other.

Together, they provide:

  • Long-lasting smooth muscle relaxation from tadalafil
  • Faster-acting blood-flow support from sildenafil

This combination can support both extended readiness and quicker responsiveness without requiring daily dosing.

The Role of Apomorphine in Sexual Performance

Apomorphine works differently from PDE5 inhibitors. It is a dopamine agonist, meaning it acts on dopamine receptors in the brain rather than blood vessels.

Apomorphine helps:

  • Boost mental arousal
  • Enhance responsiveness to stimulation

By addressing the neurological component of sexual performance, apomorphine supports desire and initiation — areas tadalafil alone does not directly influence.
