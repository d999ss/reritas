What to Try After Oral ED Pills Stop Working

Contents
March 12, 2026

Erectile dysfunction is one of the most treatable conditions in men's health — yet millions of men find themselves stuck when their first-line treatment stops delivering results. If Viagra, Cialis, or Levitra worked well at first but now seem to be falling short, you're not alone, and you're not out of options.

This guide explains why standard ED medications lose effectiveness over time, and what to consider next — including a more advanced alternative that may work when traditional pills don't.

Why Standard ED Medications Stop Working

PDE5 inhibitors like sildenafil (Viagra), tadalafil (Cialis), and vardenafil (Levitra) have helped millions of men improve their sexual performance by increasing blood flow to the penis. For many, they remain effective long-term. But for a significant number of men, results become inconsistent or insufficient over time.

Here's why.

Tolerance Build-Up

Research suggests it's possible to develop a tolerance to sildenafil and similar medications over time. When the body becomes accustomed to a drug, it may require progressively higher doses to achieve the same effect. This can occur with extended use or may be tied to changes in underlying health conditions.

Worsening Underlying Health Conditions

ED is frequently a symptom of poor vascular health. Conditions like diabetes, cardiovascular disease, obesity, and high blood pressure can all worsen ED and reduce the effectiveness of PDE5 inhibitors. If blood flow is significantly impaired, standard doses may simply not be strong enough to produce satisfactory results.

Hormonal factors also play a role. Low testosterone and other hormonal imbalances can contribute to ED in ways that PDE5 inhibitors aren't designed to address — and may require hormone therapy before oral ED medications can work effectively.

Psychogenic ED — The Factor Pills Can't Touch

Here's something many men don't realize: PDE5 inhibitors don't address the psychological side of erectile dysfunction. Performance anxiety, depression, stress, relationship issues, and other mental health factors can all cause or worsen ED — and no amount of sildenafil will resolve a problem rooted in the brain's arousal pathways.

For men whose ED has a significant psychological component, medication that targets mental arousal — alongside or instead of standard PDE5 inhibitors — is often what makes the difference.

Other Contributing Factors

Improper dosing, drug interactions, inconsistent timing, food intake, and alcohol use can all reduce the effectiveness of oral ED medications. Before concluding a medication isn't working, it's worth reviewing these variables with a healthcare provider.
