Important safety information

A once-daily pill combining tadalafil for ED and minoxidil for hair growth. Get always-on erection support while regrowing hair in as little as 3 months, two solutions in one simple routine.

For ED: Tadalafil boosts blood flow for always-on erection support whenever you need it. Take it daily and stay ready, no timing, no planning, no searching for a pill before sex.

For hair growth: Minoxidil increases blood flow and nutrients to hair follicles, helping hair grow fuller and healthier while slowing further loss. Results typically show in 12 weeks with consistent daily use.

Some people may experience mild side effects while taking this medication. The most common include headache, upset stomach or indigestion, back pain, muscle aches, nasal congestion, flushing (warmth or redness in the face), and pain in the arms or legs. Because this medication contains oral minoxidil, you may also notice increased hair growth on the face or body, mild swelling in the legs or around the eyes, lightheadedness, a faster heartbeat or palpitations, trouble sleeping, nausea, bloating, or mild fluid retention. These side effects are usually manageable, but you should let your healthcare provider know if they persist or become bothersome.

Although uncommon, more serious side effects can occur. Seek emergency medical care if you experience chest pain, shortness of breath, fainting, sudden dizziness, or symptoms of fluid buildup such as rapid weight gain or significant swelling in the face, hands, ankles, or stomach. Rarely, fluid can accumulate around the heart, which can be serious. This medication may also cause a significant drop in blood pressure, especially if taken with nitrates, certain blood pressure medications, alpha blockers, or large amounts of alcohol.

Stop taking the medication and seek immediate medical attention if you have an erection lasting longer than 4 hours, sudden loss of vision in one or both eyes, or sudden decrease or loss of hearing. These events are rare but require urgent medical care.
If you ever feel unsure about a symptom, it is always safest to contact your healthcare provider.
